A Greener Future: Gen Z Volunteer Recruitment

Project Overview

This 3-day project focuses on developing a data-driven recruitment and retention strategy for A Greener Future, which is a non-profit organization dedicated to environmental preservation. The goal was to help connect initial volunteer interest and long-term commitment, specifically, through Gen Z participants.

See Poster Board See Literature Review

Day 1: Ideation

Internal vs. External Motivations

The team analyzed how individuals decided to volunteer, drawing on a variety of sources, such as online articles and peer-reviewed research. This helped distinguish internal motivations and external influences.

Adapting HR for Non-Profits

We conducted 11 moderated sessions, each spanning 20-30 minutes, where participants reviewed current billing statements. This allowed the team to fully capture emotional triggers and identify specifics in which users felt confused.

Initial Framework

The first draft of the recruitment strategy was being developed, focusing on how organizational framing and clear expectations can overcome early hesitation in attracting potential volunteers.

Day 2: Academic Synthesis and Shareholder Alignment

Literature Review

Synthesized key findings from behavioural science to discuss volunteer motivations and utilize learning opportunities as a retention tool.

Problem First Messaging

Developed a slide deck for shareholders that highlights the environmental urgency that comes with the projects the organization holds, such as their nurdle cleans ups and the impact of nurdles on the wildlife, and the pollution cigarrete butts cause on bodies of water.

Gen Z Alignment

A targeted strategy for young volunteers was formed by identifying their core values being: authenticity, transparency, and systemic critique. The goal was to shift the organization’s identity from offering volunteer tasks to movement-building.

Barrier Negation

Develop strategies based on social connection and the visibility of impact to address recurring volunteer struggles, such as burnout and feelings of being an outsider.

Day 3: Presentation and Border Design

Poster Board Production and Presentation

We designed a visual representation that summarized our recruitment strategies and insights. We presented our strategies to A Greener Future, alongside other shareholders visiting our booth.

Retention Model

We showcased how consistent volunteering forms long-term organizational sustainability, leading to better visibility and stronger ability to maintain momentum in the community.

Future Impact and Takeaways

From Tasks to Impacts

Our final strategy suggested that retention is highest when volunteers can see the direct results of their work, such as statistics showing how much garbage was collected during a given month.

Authenticity over Marketing

Younger demographics tend to feel that traditional recruitment strategies are more transactional and less genuine; an ideal strategy must instead forge genuine community leadership and environmental justice.

Data-Driven Proofs

By integrating data collection into the volunteer experience, the organization can provide the proof of impact that volunteers see, which pushes commitment over several years.

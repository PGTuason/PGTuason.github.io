UX Case Study: Legible, A Plain Language Bill Explainer

Project Overview

Legible is a web-based dashboard designed to transform dense, jargon-heavy billing statements into easily understandable insights that are clear and visually appealing. The project looks to address the “Avoidance Cycle”, where users tend to ignore financial documents due to cognitive overload and emotional stress.

See Final Prototype

Research Design and Methodology

Concept Validation and Prioritization

The team utilized a prioritization grid to evaluate user impact and scope of what could be meaningfully prototyped. In the end, the “Bills Explainer” concept was highly regarded due to its potential to resolve user frustrations.

Informal Interviews

We conducted 11 moderated sessions, each spanning 20-30 minutes, where participants reviewed current billing statements. This allowed the team to fully capture emotional triggers and identify specifics in which users felt confused.

Synthesis Artifacts

The resulting quantitative data were then formed into a representative persona, Mia Wilson, a nursing student whose goals and frustrations guided our design decisions. Moreover, an affinity diagram was applied to categorize research into five core pain points

    Information Overload and Visual Density
    Unclear Terminology and Jargon
    Diffculty Idenfying Changes Over Time
    Trust, Transparency and Sense of Control
    Emotional Stress and Avoidance

Iterative Usability Testing

After creating the medium fidelity prototype, it was then tested with 5 participants across 6 core tasks. These sessions focused on navigation flow, the clarity of the Help page, and the effectiveness of the visual summaries.

Research Insights and Findings

During our research phase, we discovered that financial anxiety often stems from poor information desing rather than a person's lack of financial literacy

Trust

Users were hesitant to engage with financial tools unless the value proposition and privacy measures were framed clearly on the landing page.

Visual Preference

Most participants significantly preferred graphical representations, such as pie charts and trend lines, over traditional table layouts to visually represent quantified data.

Financial Terms

Technical terms can cause users to stop reading bills entirely if they are unsure of the meaning.

The Final Prototype and Future Considerations

Finally, the high-fidelity prototype was formed to create a comprehensive financial companion.

The Final Prototype

    Plain-Language Glossary: a dedicated section translating and explaining financial jargon into everyday terms.
    Visual Dashboard: Data visualization of bills that highlights changes over time, categories are also placed to separate and specify spending.
    Trust-Centred Onboarding: A landing page that utilizes narratives and visual elements to explain common frustrations while explaining the tool’s security and utility prior to data uploads.

Future Considerations

    Improved Navigation: In the future, fully integrating breadcrumb trails and consistent “back” navigation across all sub-pages, which would support error recovery.
    Improved Onboarding: Integrate tooltips and tours to guide first-time users through the dashboard and other features, further reducing cognitive load and leading to a stress-free experience.
    Refined Interactive Elements: Changing all static elements into fully interactive components to prevent user frustration in future tests.
